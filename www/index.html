Ibex Website

Web app that helps you plan your next hiking adventure

IBEX Website is a part of Ibex platform and aims to provide hikers information about popular hikes in the area.

  1. Find ideas for your next hiking adventure
    Search for a specific peak or location or find the best hikes around your current position.
  2. Get Inspired by beautiful photos
    Ibex Website collects photos of hikes from Instagram users to show you how great hiking can be.
  3. Plan your trip
    With integrated Google Maps we can give you an estimate how long the trip will take you and you can also get specific driving directions to the starting point.
    Elevation profile and hike route diagram will give you a better sense of how easy or difficult the hike itself is.
  4. Be prepared
    Where available, Ibex website will provide you useful information about weather, public transport, huts and other services you may encounter on your hike.

Ibex Website is a responsive web app, available on https://ibexapp.com/. Client app is built using angular-cli with Angular 5. Backend consists of Firebase Real-time Database and Cloud Functions. Specific effort was devoted to making the Web App as SEO friendly as possible (e.g. using meaningful links for specific hikes pages, generating robots and sitemap files, etc.). I am still working on publishing the app with server-side rendering with Angular Universal to improve initial loading time and make the site frendlier to crawlers and social sharing - which seems to be a big problem of all Angular (or any other JavaScript framework) apps.
Ibex Website is using Google Maps, Places and Elevation APIs to gather and present information about hikes.
My contribution was bulding the front-end application as well as the back-end services, based on the designs created in Invision. I was also responsible for publishing the app on the web.

SeltronHome °Clausius

iOS, Android & Web App for managing Seltron Heating Controllers

Clausius works with Seltron devices. It helps homeowners manage their heating system and get professional assistance from anywhere, anytime. Clausius lets you:

  • See how it works with virtual device configuration before purchase,
  • Login or create a new account,
  • Claim Seltron devices and assign them to multiple heating zones and domestic hot water circuits in your buildings,
  • View current outside weather and temperature,
  • View and manage home and domestic hot water temperatures remotely,
  • Switch heating modes and heating functions remotely,
  • Configure and personalize home heating schedules,
  • View and read heating system notifications and alerts, and
  • Give access to other users and maintainers and allow them to configure your heating system.

Current Clausius application is built using Ionic1 framework and therefore based on Angular1 framework. Amongst other technologies, it takes advantage of auth0.js client for social and username/password authentication, i18next.js for internationalization, SignalR client over websockets for real-time data updates, hockeyapp for collecting and managing user feedback, and code-push for instant app updates bypassing Google Play or App Store. One of the components I am personally most proud of, is the daily time program edit component that allows users to easily adjust their heating schedule. This component is based on jquery-ui resizable and draggable elements in combination with jquery-touch-punch library for touch-enabled devices.

SeltronHome °Kelvin

iOS, Android & Web App for configuring and maintaining Seltron Heating Controllers

Kelvin application allows profesional servicemen and maintainers to remotely monitor, manage and diagnose Seltron heating controllers, thus saving them time and money and increasing customer satisfaction. Kelvin enables:

  • view and manage your clients;
  • view and manage client resources (buildings and heating controllers);
  • view current state and history of temperature sensor readings and relay states;
  • remote diagnostics;
  • remote parameters configuration and auditing log of all changes;
  • instantly view heating system warnings and notifications.

Kelvin application is built using the latest Ionic3 framework and therefore based on Angular5 framework. Like Clausius, it takes advantage of auth0.js client for social and username/password authentication, SignalR client over websockets for real-time data updates, hockeyapp for collecting and managing user feedback, and code-push for instant app updates bypassing Google Play or App Store. First we have developed a desktop/tablet optimised application and currently, we are working on a mobile application as well. A well-structured component-based-approach is enabling us to re-use the GUI components as much as possible. It also uses nvd3 charting library for graphical presentation of sensor readings and relay states which was built by a colleague of mine.

Seltron FW Update

Android App for remotely updating Seltron Heating controllers firmware over USB.

Seltron Regulators sometimes receive either crytical software updates or new features. To make thing seasier for users and their servicemen, we have built this Android mobile app that allows trained professionals to update heating controllers firmware via USB OTG cable on the spot.

First, the heating controller serial number is scanned and validated in the company database. This gives us a list of compatible firmware versions that can be installed. Once selected, the app copies firmware and user interface images to the device memory in order to update it to the specific version.

Due to iOS limitations to access USB port, this app is currently only available for Android devices. It uses Cordova Chrome USB plugin to communicate with heating controller in bootloader mode. Unfortunately, due to Seltron Company Policy app is not publicly available on Google Play store.

Since the first version, this app was taken over by a colleauge of mine at Nomnio, who is working on supporting additional heating controllers and further improving user experience.
